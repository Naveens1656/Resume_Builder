## Best Practices

### Contact Information
✅ **DO:**
- Include full name, professional email, phone number
- Add location (city, state) - no full address needed
- Include LinkedIn profile if optimized
- Add portfolio/website for creative/tech roles

❌ **DON'T:**
- Include age, marital status, photo (in US)
- Use unprofessional email addresses
- List full street address

### Professional Summary
✅ **DO:**
- Keep to 2-3 sentences (30-80 words)
- Include quantifiable achievements
- Use industry keywords from job description
- Highlight unique value proposition

❌ **DON'T:**
- Use generic phrases ("hard worker", "team player")
- Write in first person ("I am...")
- Exceed 100 words
- Include objectives instead of value

### Work Experience
✅ **DO:**
- Start each bullet with action verbs
- Include specific metrics (%, $, numbers)
- Focus on achievements, not duties
- Keep bullets to 1-2 lines
- List 3-5 achievements per role

**Example:**
- ❌ Weak: "Responsible for managing sales team"
- ✅ Strong: "Led team of 12 sales representatives, increasing regional revenue by 45% ($3.2M) in 18 months"

❌ **DON'T:**
- Use passive language
- List job duties without impact
- Include irrelevant positions
- Go back more than 10-15 years

### Education
✅ **DO:**
- List most recent degree first
- Include GPA if 3.5+ (optional)
- Add honors and awards
- Include relevant coursework for recent graduates

❌ **DON'T:**
- Include high school if you have a degree
- List graduation year if 15+ years ago
- Include GPA if below 3.0

### Skills
✅ **DO:**
- List 5-8 technical skills
- Include 3-5 professional skills
- Add relevant certifications
- Use exact keywords from job posting
- Group skills logically

❌ **DON'T:**
- Rate skills with bars/percentages (not ATS-friendly)
- Include basic skills (Microsoft Word)
- Use vague terms ("proficient in...")
- List outdated technologies

## ATS Optimization Guide

### What is ATS?
Applicant Tracking Systems are software used by 75%+ of employers to screen resumes before human review.

### ATS-Friendly Formatting

✅ **DO:**
- Use standard section headings
- Stick to simple formatting
- Save as .docx or .pdf
- Use standard fonts (10-12pt)
- Avoid headers/footers
- Spell out acronyms on first use
- Use bullet points (•)

❌ **DON'T:**
- Use tables or text boxes
- Add images, graphics, or logos
- Use columns or complex layouts
- Include special characters
- Use creative section titles
- Embed graphics in headers

### Keyword Optimization
1. **Analyze the job description**
2. **Identify key requirements and skills**
3. **Mirror the language** used in the posting
4. **Include keywords naturally** in:
   - Professional summary
   - Work achievements
   - Skills section

## Quantification Framework

Transform weak bullets into strong achievements:

### The CAR Method
**Context + Action + Result**

Example:
"Developed marketing campaign (Action) for new product launch (Context), generating $2.3M in revenue and 450 new customers in Q1 (Result)"

### Metrics to Include
- **Percentages**: "Increased efficiency by 40%"
- **Dollar amounts**: "Generated $5.2M in sales"
- **Time saved**: "Reduced processing time from 4 hours to 45 minutes"
- **Team size**: "Led team of 15 across 3 departments"
- **Volume**: "Managed portfolio of 200+ client accounts"
- **Frequency**: "Processed 500+ requests weekly"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. One-Size-Fits-All Resume
- ❌ Sending same resume to every job
- ✅ Customize for each position (15-20 minutes)

### 2. Typos and Errors
- ❌ Spelling mistakes, grammatical errors
- ✅ Proofread 3+ times, use spell-check, have someone review

### 3. Too Long or Too Short
- ❌ 3+ pages for mid-level roles OR 1 page for executives
- ✅ 1 page (0-5 years), 2 pages (5-15 years), 2-3 pages (15+ years)

### 4. Inconsistent Formatting
- ❌ Mixed fonts, sizes, spacing, date formats
- ✅ Consistent style throughout

### 5. Missing Keywords
- ❌ Using different terminology than job posting
- ✅ Mirror job description language

### 6. Focusing on Duties vs. Achievements
- ❌ "Responsible for customer service"
- ✅ "Resolved 200+ support tickets weekly with 98% satisfaction rating"

## Tips for Maximum Impact

### The 6-Second Rule
Recruiters spend an average of 6 seconds on initial resume scan. Make sure:
- Contact info is prominent
- Job titles stand out
- Key achievements are visible
- Layout is clean and scannable

### Power Words That Get Noticed
- Achieved, Exceeded, Surpassed (results)
- Managed, Led, Directed (leadership)
- Developed, Created, Designed (innovation)
- Increased, Improved, Optimized (improvement)
- Generated, Produced, Delivered (outcomes)

### The Rule of Three
For each position, highlight:
1. A leadership/management achievement
2. A quantifiable result
3. A process improvement or innovation

## Legal & Ethical Considerations

### What NOT to Include
- Age or date of birth
- Marital status
- Children or family information
- Photo (in US/Canada)
- Social Security Number
- Religious affiliation
- Political affiliations
- Health information
- Salary history (in many states)

### Honesty Policy
- Never fabricate experience or credentials
- Don't exaggerate job titles
- Be accurate with dates
- Verify all certifications are current
- Don't claim others' achievements
